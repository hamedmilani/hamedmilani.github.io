Hi, Welcome to my Page!

I am an incoming Ph.D. student in Operations and Supply Chain Management at the Haskayne School of Business, University of Calgary, advised by Dr. Marco Bijvank and Dr. Hossein Piri. Before this, I pursued Ph.D. studies in Operations Management at Ko√ß University and earned my M.Sc. in Industrial Engineering from Sharif University of Technology. I am interested in designing mathematical models that capture real-world healthcare problems and building algorithms to turn those models into actionable insights. My research combines Operations Research and Statistics to improve decision-making under uncertainty, especially in healthcare and supply chains applications. I am currently working on a project about breast cancer screening with two main parts. The first part focuses on evaluating the trade-offs and cost-effectiveness of public-level screening strategies, using a microsimulation tool. The second part is to develop a personalized framework that jointly optimizes screening and diagnosis based on individual risk factors, using approximate dynamic programming. We collaborate with co-authors from the Cumming School of Medicine and work closely with the Canadian Partnership Against Cancer (CPAC). Outside of research, I love cinema, literature, and metal music. I blog in English and Farsi, and I would be happy if you visit and share your thoughts!
